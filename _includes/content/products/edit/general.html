Here's a list of all the fields you will see on the Product Details tab

Name Product title that appears on the webpage.
Slug Automatically populated based on the name of the product when you created the product originally but you can adjust it to your needs at any time.
Description Shown on the products’ webpage as overview information. This where you can specify unique qualities and attributes about your product that’s not listed in the other fields.
Price The default price for the product.
Cost Price The default price for the product.
Available On Date module allows you to postdate your item at anytime. This will keep the product from being listed or displayed in any way on your site until the date you select - you may leave this section blank otherwise.
Promotable Determines whether or not promotions can apply to this product.
SKU Defined as a set of letters and numbers given to a product by a seller. Typically, the letters and numbers in a SKU are abbreviated attributes that distinguish one product from another product such as manufacturer, description, model, material, size, color, packaging, and warranty terms. The letter and numbers may be read by employees, or rendered as a barcode to be scanned. Not to be confused with UPC.

reference

Shipping Category Defines how the product you are selling will be delivered to the customer

Default - Standard product to be shipped.

Digital Goods - Product to be delivered electronically.
Tax Category Defines what taxes if any will be applied to your product

None - No taxes will be applied to the sale of this product.

Merchandise Default - The default tax setting for your country.
Taxons Taxonomies - Set up what all categories a product can fall under. Used to find specific types of products by narrowing search results or filtering collections pages - Brand, Category, Tags, etc...
Option Types How your product can be categorized in terms of purchasing options, these will set apart variants from their master product. Once you create an Option Type, you can create multiple Option Values for it. All the products on your site will have access to these Option Types you create.
SEO

Meta Title - What are the product title(s) that could apply.

Meta Keywords - What words, when searched for on your site or on search engines, would you want your product to be seen in results for.

Meta Descriptions - What are the product description(s) that could apply. This section is popularly scanned by search so be sure to include any relevant information while staying moderately concise.
